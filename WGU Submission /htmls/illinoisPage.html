Illinois, the 21st state of the U.S., was admitted to the Union in 1818. It is located in the Midwest and is home to both large urban cities like Chicago and vast rural areas. With a population of over 12 million, Illinois plays a significant role in U.S. agriculture, industry, and culture. It is known for its diverse geography, ranging from the shores of Lake Michigan to the fertile farmlands in the south.

Fun Facts

  • Illinois is known as the "Prairie State" due to its vast prairies and flat landscapes.
  • The first McDonald's restaurant was founded in Des Plaines, Illinois, in 1955.
  • The state is the birthplace of President Abraham Lincoln, who served as the 16th President of the United States.
  • Illinois is home to the world's first atomic reactor, which was built at the University of Chicago in 1942.
