Chicago, the third-largest city in the United States, was incorporated in 1837. Known for its rich history, diverse culture, and iconic architecture, Chicago serves as a major financial, cultural, and transportation hub in the Midwest. With a population of over 2.7 million, it is one of the most prominent cities in the country.

Fun Facts

  • Chicago is home to the world's first skyscraper, the Home Insurance Building, built in 1885.
  • The city is known for its deep-dish pizza, which originated in Chicago in the 1940s.
  • Chicago's "L" is one of the oldest and busiest rapid transit systems in the country.
  • The Chicago River is dyed green every year on St. Patrick's Day.
